Supply chain management involves the oversight and coordination of all activities involved in sourcing, procurement, conversion, and logistics management. It encompasses the movement and storage of raw materials, work-in-process inventory, and finished goods from point of origin to point of consumption.

Supply chain disruptions, as dramatically illustrated by the COVID-19 pandemic, can have severe consequences for businesses and economies. The pandemic exposed vulnerabilities in global supply chains, including over-reliance on single suppliers and regions, lack of inventory buffers, and limited visibility into supply chain operations.

Inventory management is a critical aspect of supply chain management. Holding too much inventory ties up capital and incurs storage costs, while holding too little risks stockouts and lost sales. Techniques like just-in-time inventory and safety stock calculations help optimize inventory levels.

Demand forecasting uses historical data and predictive models to estimate future demand for products. Accurate demand forecasts help organizations plan production, manage inventory, and allocate resources efficiently. AI and machine learning are increasingly used to improve the accuracy of demand forecasts.

Supplier relationship management involves developing and maintaining productive relationships with suppliers. Organizations must evaluate suppliers based on factors like quality, reliability, cost, and sustainability. Strong supplier relationships can lead to better prices, priority during shortages, and innovation collaboration.

Logistics and transportation management involves planning and optimizing the movement of goods. Route optimization, load planning, and carrier selection are key tasks. AI and IoT are transforming logistics by enabling real-time tracking, dynamic routing, and predictive maintenance.

Sustainable supply chain management considers the environmental and social impact of supply chain activities. Organizations are under increasing pressure from consumers, investors, and regulators to reduce their carbon footprint, ensure fair labor practices, and promote responsible sourcing.

Supply chain technology platforms integrate data from across the supply chain to provide visibility and support decision-making. Enterprise resource planning systems, warehouse management systems, and transportation management systems are key components of supply chain technology infrastructure.
