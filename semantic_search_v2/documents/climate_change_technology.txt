Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity. Rising global temperatures, caused primarily by the emission of greenhouse gases, are leading to more frequent and severe weather events, rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems and agriculture.

Renewable energy technologies like solar and wind power are crucial for reducing greenhouse gas emissions from the electricity sector. The cost of solar panels and wind turbines has fallen dramatically over the past decade, making them competitive with fossil fuels in many parts of the world.

Energy storage technologies, particularly batteries, are essential for addressing the intermittency of renewable energy sources. Lithium-ion batteries are widely used in electric vehicles and grid-scale storage. Research is ongoing into more advanced battery technologies with higher energy density and lower cost.

Carbon capture and storage, or CCS, involves capturing carbon dioxide emissions from power plants and industrial facilities and storing them underground. While promising, CCS technology remains expensive and has not been deployed at the scale needed to make a significant impact on global emissions.

Electric vehicles reduce emissions from the transportation sector, which is a major source of greenhouse gases. The adoption of electric vehicles is accelerating, driven by falling battery costs, government incentives, and improvements in charging infrastructure.

Smart grid technology uses digital communication and control systems to make the electricity grid more efficient, reliable, and flexible. Smart grids can integrate large amounts of renewable energy, enable demand response, and reduce energy waste.

Precision agriculture uses technology including sensors, drones, and AI to optimize farming practices and reduce the environmental impact of agriculture. By applying inputs like water, fertilizer, and pesticides more precisely, farmers can reduce waste and improve yields.

International cooperation is essential for addressing climate change. The Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015, set a goal of limiting global warming to well below two degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels. Countries are required to submit nationally determined contributions outlining their plans to reduce emissions.
